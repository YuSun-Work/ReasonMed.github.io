Abstract

We introduce ReasonMed, the largest open-source medical reasoning dataset, comprising 370 K rigorously validated chain-of-thought examples. Leveraging a multi-agent system of three competitive LLMs, we generated 1.75 M reasoning paths and applied a three-tier verification and refinement pipeline to ensure correctness, coherence, and factuality. We then systematically compare fine-tuning strategies—explicit CoT, concise summarization, and a hybrid approach—on medical QA benchmarks. Our hybrid method achieves state-of-the-art performance among sub-10B models, demonstrating the value of explicit reasoning in knowledge-intensive domains.
